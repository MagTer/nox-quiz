The n0x mark — six directions

The current title is the NOX RUN wordmark. These shorten it to n0x in the game's dark-grunge palette — six treatments across color, case, and glyph idea. All pixel-baked from the same vendored font. Pick one (or a blend); the final title hero + select badge get baked from it and go through your sign-off — this is direction-picking, not the finished asset.

1

Heritage

moss green + neon outline·lowercase

Closest to the current NOX RUN mark — same moss/neon-green DNA, just tighter and shortened. The safe evolution.

2

Ember

rust + ember glow·lowercase

Pulls the mark into the late-game castle heat — warm, harsh, a little dangerous. Reads as the harder edge of the world.

3

Steel

steel-blue + bone outline·uppercase

Colder and more upright — an arcade-cabinet read. The uppercase N0X feels sturdier, more machine than moss.

4

Mono-grunge

bone white + scanlines·uppercase

Color-neutral and the most distressed — heavy scanline wear and a hard drop shadow. Lets the biome art carry all the color.

5

Neon-zero

muted mark, glowing 0·lowercase

The one real idea: the zero is the hero. n and x recede in grey while the 0 burns neon-green — makes the glyph itself the brand hook.

6

Stamp-badge

riveted metal plate·lowercase

An emblem, not a wordmark — n0x stamped into a distressed riveted plate. Aimed at the level-select badge more than the title hero.

Tell me which way to go. Reply with a number or name — or mix them (e.g. “Heritage color, but the Neon-zero glowing-0 idea”).
