# Phase 34.6 Plan 02: Rebuild level-01 (calm swamp intro) Summary

**Rebuilt `src/levels/level-01.js` from scratch as a ~2x-length, ceiling-free calm swamp intro (goal.x 3560 -> 7100) with a deliberate ascent/descent hump and an optional visible high route, catching and fixing two real driven-player-only defects (a ceiling-bonked spike-hop and several spike-before-gap-takeoff conflicts) that the static validator could not see.**

- Rebuilt `src/levels/level-01.js` entirely (append-only convention explicitly suspended for this phase): 11 floor runs, 9 platforms (all `h: 16`, all off-grid `y`, zero overlapping pairs), 34 coins, 8 spikes, exactly one door, one enemy, `mathGates: []`, one secret alcove, no `bounds` field.

## Decisions Made
- Kept `displayName: "The First Descent"` — the name already fits the new literal descent (the ascent/descent hump), so no rename was needed.
- The optional high route achieves verticality in OPEN AIR (zero platform-pair x-overlap anywhere in the level) rather than via a real ceiling, per 34.6-RESEARCH.md's documented Pitfall 1 reconciliation of "even levels are vertical" against LEVEL-DESIGN §8's L1-2 "no ceilings at all" rule — level-01 is an odd (calm) level, so this reconciliation applies even more strongly here than it will on level-02.
- All 34 coins use walk-family placement (`coin.y = surfaceY - 56`) exclusively rather than mixing in jump-family arcs, guaranteeing every coin is a trivially-collectable fly-through box with zero reachability ambiguity — verified end-to-end by `audit-coins.mjs`.
- Spike placement now follows a >=250px-from-floor-edge margin uniformly (matching the original shipped level-01's own convention), rather than the tighter ~80-120px margins used in the first draft, after the in-engine harness proved the tighter margins caused genuine driven-player failures (see Deviations below).

**2. [Rule 1 - Bug] Spikes too close to a gap/mount takeoff (spike-before-gap conflict class)**
- **Found during:** Task 1, second and third `browser-boot.mjs` verification passes
- **Issue:** Several spikes were originally placed only ~80-120px before a floor's edge (a bare-gap or platform-mount jump takeoff). `scripts/lib/route-planner.mjs`'s documented spike-before-mount conflict-merge fix only covers platform mounts, not bare gap takeoffs — a spike whose own hop lands near a gap's takeoff zone can strand the driven player mid-air while the gap-jump's narrow fire window passes underneath, unfired. - **Fix:** Repositioned every spike in the level with a >=250px margin from its floor's own edge (matching the original shipped level-01's proven-safe convention), and updated the corresponding checkpoints to keep an 80px lead before each relocated spike.
- **Files modified:** `src/levels/level-01.js`

- **Committed in:** `c417d49` (part of Task 1's commit)

## Checkpoint rework (2026-07-15, Opus 4.8)

**Human checkpoint note:** *"Level 1 is a bit too much of a soft landing."* The first
(Sonnet-model) 34.6-02 cut was validator-legal but characterless — one ascent/descent
hump up front, then a long near-monotonous ground-level coin-walk to the goal. This
rework, on a stronger model, brings real level-design judgment to the calm intro:
gentle, ROLLING terrain instead of a flat walk, while keeping it clearly the gentlest
of the 8 levels and clearly distinct from level-02's new tall mandatory switchback climb.

### What changed (floor / platform / coin / checkpoint / spike geometry only)

Preserved as fixed landmarks (not moved): **door@1300, enemy@2900, goal@7100 (~2x
length), secret alcove@320, swamp biome, allowedTables [6-9], no key** (odd archetype).
Only the terrain geometry around those landmarks was reworked.

**Gentle verticality added (the core fix), spread ACROSS the level:**

- **Two gentle up-and-over HUMPS** (was one). Each is a small stepped ascent → peak →
  stepped **descent** that bridges a wide gap no bare jump can cross:
  - HUMP 1 bridges the 410px F2→F3 gap (H1a rise 70 → H1b peak rise 65 → H1c descent
    drop 65 → down to F3), landing the player just before the enemy.
  - HUMP 2 bridges the 390px F5→F6 gap (H2a rise 70 → H2b peak rise 65 → H2c descent
    drop 65 → down to F6).
  So the up-and-**down** is a real part of the journey, not decoration, and each hump's
  far side is a deliberate descent (peak → lower tier → floor). That is **two genuine
  stepped ascents + two deliberate descents**.
- **One short OPTIONAL, fully-visible HIGH ROUTE** over F2 (HR1 rise 64 → HR2 rise 64,
  two non-overlapping tiers) carrying **3 bonus coins** — inviting, never mandatory,
  never a key. Skipping it costs only those three coins.
- **One optional final MOUND (PD)** on the goal run (rise 64, walk-off descent) so even
  the run-in rolls instead of lying flat.

That is **three step-up ascents visible to the player (2 mandatory humps + the optional
high route) plus the optional mound**, and **three-plus deliberate descents** (each
hump's far side + the mound walk-off + the high-route drop-back).

**Still clearly the gentlest / forgiveness lever confirmed:**

- Floor gaps pulled to a **forgiving, varied 120 / 140px** — comfortably shorter than
  the 160px comfort max and the ~162px bare running-jump reach. (Down from the first
  cut, which mixed in 160px and 410px spans; the wide spans now exist ONLY as
  hump-bridged, never as a bare required jump.)
- **On "gaps at marginRatio=1.000":** investigated and confirmed this is the documented
  **WR-01 validator artifact**, NOT "gaps at the maximum jump distance." Every floor sits
  at FLOOR_Y (320) — `build.js` pins floors, so floors cannot vary in height — hence every
  floor→floor hop is FLAT (Δy=0), and per `reachability.mjs` lines 244-258 a flat/downward
  hop's `marginRatio` is *always* exactly 1.000 regardless of how short the gap is (the
  degenerate single-root case). The metric literally cannot report a flat gap as "easy,"
  so it cannot be "pulled back." The real forgiveness lever — the physical gap **width** —
  IS pulled back, to a slack 120/140px. Genuine sub-1.0 margins only appear on the RISING
  hops onto the new step-up tiers, and those are all gentle 64-70px rises (well inside the
  88.331px envelope) — that is where the "pulled back from the max edge" actually shows.
- **NO ceilings anywhere** — ZERO overlapping platform pairs (the L1-2 §8 rule); every
  rise is in the 60-70px non-overlapping band, never the 72-75 climb band.
- **Spikes cut 8 → 5** (deliberately sparing for the gentlest level), each centered on a
  clear dedicated floor with a **≥250px margin from its right (gap/mount takeoff) edge**
  and clear of every platform's x-footprint (no ceiling-bonk), each behind a checkpoint.
  None on the opening run (F0/F1) or the final approach (F9) — the welcome and the run-in
  stay calm.

**Distinct from level-02:** level-01 rolls gently along the ground (all floors at
FLOOR_Y, gentle optional/humped verticality, gaps ≤140); level-02 is a tall MANDATORY
switchback climb to y:-50 (370px) with a key apex. The contrast is the point.
