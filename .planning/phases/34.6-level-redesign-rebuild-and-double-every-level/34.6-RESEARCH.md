The one code change is making the end-of-level gate key-conditional: on levels that place `geometry.keys` (no `geometry.locks`), a held key clears the level with full XP INSTEAD of opening the end math challenge. The validation story is the phase's real risk surface. The rebuild DELETES all 13 currently-deferred Phase-34 HARD-FAILs (8 `coin-reachability` on levels 01–03, 5 `headroom` on level-07) because it replaces the geometry they live on — `validate-levels.mjs` must land at **zero HARD-FAIL**. The static key-lock softlock validator (`scripts/lib/key-lock-check.mjs`) is `?? **Primary recommendation:** Split the phase around the mandated mid-phase human-verify. Structure: (A) land the key-conditional end-gate code + its two-path audit, (B) rebuild levels 1+2 (calm swamp intro + intense/vertical swamp with the first math-skip key), (C) `checkpoint:human-verify` on the two-level prototype shape, (D) rebuild levels 3–8 to the proven shape. ## Level-Author Hard Constraints (the numbers a planner hands to authors)

All `[VERIFIED: docs/LEVEL-DESIGN.md + src/config.js, live tree 2026-07-15]`. The physics envelope is frozen calibration (`scripts/lib/jump-envelope.mjs`, measured against the real engine at RUN_SPEED=240, GRAVITY=1400, JUMP_FORCE=520 — all confirmed in `src/config.js`).

### Physics envelope (the ceiling everything derives from)
| Quantity | Value | Rule |
|----------|-------|------|
| Standing-jump rise (design budget) | **maxRise = 88.331px** (`JUMP_ENVELOPE.maxRise`) | **HARD:** never require a single hop rising more than 88.331px — `validate-levels.mjs`'s Δy-aware BFS HARD-FAILs it |
| Running-jump flat reach | **~162px** | **HARD:** never require a bare gap wider than ~162px |
| Player collider | **16×32, locked** (`src/player.js`) | independent of animation frame; every clearance derives from the 32px height |

### Floors and gaps
- Floors are `{ x, w }` pinned to `FLOOR_Y = 320`. Floors and gaps snap to the 16px tile grid. (Platform tier `y` deliberately does NOT snap — see grid trap.)
- **SOFT:** standard gap widths **120 / 140 / 160px** (120 easy, 160 comfortable max for a bare running jump).
- **HARD:** a gap wider than 160px must be bridged by a platform.
- **HARD (over-hole rule):** NEVER place a door / mathGate / enemy over a gap — its full-height blocker must stand on solid floor. Gate: `over-hole` rows in `validate-levels.mjs`.

### Platforms (raised ledges)
- **HARD:** platform thickness is **`h: 16`** (WYSIWYG — the collider equals the drawn 16px ledge). ALL platforms in the rebuild are `h: 16`. (53 of 59 shipped platforms are the wrong `h: 24` — that is exactly what the rebuild fixes; do NOT carry `h: 24` forward.)
- **HARD (headroom ≥ 24px)** for any two platforms overlapping in x (upper tier forming a ceiling over a lower walk):
  ```
  headroom = (lower surface y) − (upper platform y + upper platform h) − 32   ≥ 24px
  ```
  With `h: 16` this simplifies to **`rise ≥ 72px` for any overlapping tier pair.** Gate: `headroom` rows in `validate-levels.mjs`.
- **Rise bands:**
  - Overlapping tiers (ceiling above): **72–75px** (pinned between the 72px headroom floor and the 75px comfort ceiling).
  - Non-overlapping tiers (open air above): **60–70px** (the gentler band).
  - **HARD ceiling always:** never exceed **88.331px**.
- **Grid-snapping trap (READ):** do NOT snap a climb tier's `y` to the 16px grid. 64px rise → 16px headroom (too cramped, fails); 80px rise → 90% of max jump (too punishing). 72–75px is intentionally off-grid.
- **x-overlap between consecutive climb tiers: ~70px** (20–30px produced false spawn-goal HARD-FAILs on 07/08). Don't add stepping stones inside an independently-jumpable gap (intercepts a held running jump). Keep platform undersides out of spike-hop arcs.

### Math barriers (density LOCKED)
- **Density LOCKED at 1 door + 1 enemy + end gate = 3 challenges/level.** A pacing deviation is NEVER fixed by adding/removing a barrier. `mathGates: []` on every level (all mid-level checkpoint gates removed 2026-07-12).
- **SOFT spacing between consecutive barriers: 300–750px.** Closer than ~300px reads as a wall of interruptions. >1000px barrier-free stretches are fine breathers.
- **HARD (mechanic reachability):** every door/enemy must be reachable from spawn per the validator, and the interactive audit must show `triggered: true` for every encounter.

### Checkpoints
- **HARD:** one checkpoint near spawn, one just before EVERY spike/hazard cluster.
- **SOFT:** keep ≤700px between checkpoints on hazard-bearing stretches (shipped norm 150–700px).

### Secret alcove (exactly one per level)
- 24×24 (`CONFIG.ALCOVE_SIZE`) invisible walk-through trigger, ~70px above an early/mid-level platform as one extra optional hop — off the required path, never signposted, never gating, costs nothing to skip.
- Gate: `secret-alcove-reachability` row in `validate-levels.mjs` + `audit-phase21-mechanics.mjs` entity-destroy/XP-delta signal.

### Camera bounds (the trap)
- **level-01 DERIVES its right edge from geometry** (`game.js` computes `Math.max(...floors, ...platforms, goal.x+GOAL_SIZE)`); it carries NO `bounds` field.
- **level-02+ carry an explicit `bounds` field used AS-IS** — you MUST hand-bump `bounds.right` when extending, or the camera clamps short of the new goal.
- Play space stays within y 0–360 for floors; a climb may go negative (`bounds.top: -360` on levels 07/08; level-08 summit at y −125). Fall-respawn line is the GLOBAL `LEVEL_BOTTOM(360) + FALL_MARGIN(120)`, not per-level bounds.

## Key-Conditional End Gate (the ONE code change)

`[VERIFIED: game.js onReachGoal (lines 214–278), mathGate.js, key.js, live tree]`

### What already ships (reuse verbatim, no change)

### What to change
Today `onReachGoal()` (lines 214–277) unconditionally: fire-once latch → zero velocity + `player.paused = true` → `openMathGate({ brain, onClear({ table }) { ...award XP, markCleared, hud, burst, save, go("select") } })`.

The key-conditional change:
1. **Extract the level-clear body** (the current `onClear` inner block: `progress.addXp`, `progress.markCleared`, `hud.refresh`/`flashLevelUp`, `fx.clearBurst`, `writeSave`, the `clearTransitionTween` → `go("select")`) into a single closure function, e.g. `clearLevel({ table })`, so BOTH paths share it and cannot drift.
2. **Branch in `onReachGoal` BEFORE `openMathGate`:** if a key is held (`heldKeyIds.size > 0`, equivalently `hasKey(null)` for the single-key case), skip the challenge and call the shared clear directly — plus render the "LEVEL CLEAR" banner + `audio.playSfx("clear")` that `mathGate.js onSuccess` normally provides (lines 37–56), since the math path won't run. Otherwise call `openMathGate(...)` exactly as today.
3. **Full XP on the key path** — see the OPEN QUESTION below: the math path awards `progress.addXp(table)` for the answered table (`XP_EASY 10` / `XP_HARD 20`). The key-skip path has no answered table, so the planner must choose the XP value for "full credit."

**Why the branch belongs in `game.js`, not `mathGate.js`:** `heldKeyIds` is scene-closure run-state; `mathGate.js` has no reference to it and passing it in widens the seam's API unnecessarily. `[VERIFIED: game.js closure structure]`

**Odd levels are automatically unaffected:** they place no `keys`, so `heldKeyIds` is always empty → the branch always falls through to `openMathGate`. No per-level flag needed. Even levels place exactly one optional key (no `keyId` → shares `KEY_DEFAULT_ID`), so `heldKeyIds.size > 0` is a sound "did she find this level's key" test.

### OPEN QUESTION — full-XP value on the key-skip path `[ASSUMED]`
The math path calls `progress.addXp(table)` where `table` is the answered question's operand (10 or 20 XP by table band). The key path has no `table`. "FULL level-clear XP/credit" (CONTEXT) most naturally means award the same XP the end gate would have — but the end gate's XP is table-dependent (which table the brain happened to draw). Simplest defensible readings: (a) award `XP_HARD` (20) as a flat "full" value; (b) draw a representative table from `level.allowedTables` and award its band. Both satisfy "no penalty." This must be a locked decision — flag for the discuss/checkpoint. Note `progress.markCleared(level.id)` (unlock derivation) is XP-independent and MUST fire on both paths regardless.

## Keys-Without-Locks: builder + validator tolerance (VERIFIED clean)

CONTEXT requires `geometry.keys` WITHOUT `geometry.locks`. Both the builder and the softlock validator handle this cleanly `[VERIFIED: build.js + key-lock-check.mjs + validate-levels.mjs, live tree]`:

- **`build.js`:** `for (const l of g.locks ?? [])` (line 363) → emits ZERO lock entities when `locks` is absent. `for (const k of g.keys ?? [])` (line 399) → emits the key normally. No coupling; a key with no lock is a free-standing walk-through pickup. ✓
- **`key-lock-check.mjs` `checkKeyLockReachability`:** `const locks = geometry.locks ?? []` (line 194), `const keys = geometry.keys ?? []` (line 191). The per-lock loop (`locks.forEach`) does not execute when `locks` is empty → returns `{ rows: [], hardFailCount: 0 }`. The module's own self-test **case (e)** asserts exactly this: no keys/locks → never throws, returns the empty-clean contract. A **keys-only, lock-free level produces ZERO key-lock rows and cannot HARD-FAIL** on the softlock check. ✓
- **Consequence:** the softlock validator neither fails nor "warns" on a math-skip key — it is silent for it (correct: an optional key with no lock cannot soft-lock). The static proof that the key is REACHABLE at all still comes from the general `checkLevelReachability` (the key point isn't specifically reachability-checked as a key, but the platform/route it sits on is a normal reachability node). If the planner wants a positive static assertion that the key's high route is reachable, note that the key `{x,y}` is not currently modeled by any point-reachability check (only locks trigger `key-lock-check`). ## Two-Path Audit Requirement (the key-skip proof)

`[VERIFIED: audit-key-lock.mjs, browser-boot.mjs, mechanic-drive.mjs signatures, live tree]`

The static model is not sufficient (milestone precedent: sawtooth floor, grey ground, ceiling-bonk coins, 9px headroom all shipped past green static gates). CONTEXT requires proving BOTH end-gate paths with a real driven player:

- **Path A (key collected → free clear):** a driven player walks the high route, collects the key (`get("key").length` drops to 0 — the ONLY `destroy` of a `"key"` tag is `key.js`'s pickup), reaches the goal, and the level clears **WITHOUT the math challenge ever opening**. Signal: `get("challenge").length` stays at baseline (0 opened) through goal contact, while the clear happens (the shared clear path renders the `"gate-cleared"`-tagged banner and/or the scene transitions to `select`). Distinguish "cleared via key" from "cleared via math" by: challenge count never incremented + clear marker present.
- **Path B (key skipped → math required):** a driven player who does NOT divert to the key reaches the goal and the **math challenge DOES open** (`get("challenge").length` increments — the same baseline-relative `triggered` signal `driveToXPlanned` already returns).

### Pattern: extract-and-share the clear path
**What:** Move the `onClear` body into a named closure `clearLevel({ table })` so the math path (`openMathGate`'s `onClear`) and the key path both call it. **When to use:** the moment two call sites need identical clear behavior. **Why:** prevents XP/save divergence between paths (the exact drift risk of duplicating the block).

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- **Editing `build.js` for content.** Levels are pure data; content never touches the builder (LEVEL-DESIGN §9.1). The builder already emits everything needed.
- **Carrying `h: 24` platforms forward.** The rebuild's platforms are ALL `h: 16` (WYSIWYG). `h: 24` = 8px invisible collider below the drawn ledge.
- **Snapping climb-tier `y` to 16px.** Forces 64px (too cramped) or 80px (too punishing) rises.
- **A stepping stone inside an independently-jumpable gap** — intercepts a held running jump mid-flight even when the validator reads the gap as fine.
- **Adding/removing a barrier to fix pacing.** Density LOCKED at 1 door + 1 enemy + end gate.
- **Engine global at module top level** (a727c13). Descriptors reference NO engine globals (they import only `../config.js`). `index.js` must stay node-importable.
- **Any timer/scheduler** (`setTimeout`/`setInterval`/`wait()`/`loop()`/`lifespan()`) or punishment construct — banned everywhere in `src/` (`check-safety.sh`).

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: "Vertical" read as "ceilinged" on level-02
**What goes wrong:** CONTEXT says even levels (incl. level-02) are "intense and vertical," but LEVEL-DESIGN §8 says L1–2 have **NO overlapping tiers (no ceilings) at all** — that is the lever making level-01/02 forgiving. Naively adding a ceiling to level-02 to make it "vertical" violates §8 and the ramp.
**Why:** verticality (tall climbs, descents, a high route for the key) does NOT require an upper tier forming a ceiling over a lower walk. The headroom rule only bites on OVERLAPPING tiers.
**How to avoid:** on level-02 achieve intensity/verticality with tall single-tier climbs and descents in **open air** (60–70px rises, no overlapping pairs). The key's high route uses non-overlapping tiers. Reserve real ceilings for L3–6 (appearing) and L7–8 (tight). Surface this reconciliation at the 1-2 checkpoint.
**Warning signs:** `validate-levels.mjs` emits `headroom` rows on level-02 (there should be none — L1–2 have zero overlapping pairs).

### Pitfall 2: Forgetting bounds.right after lengthening
**What goes wrong:** level-02..08 use `bounds` AS-IS. Doubling length without bumping `bounds.right` clamps the camera short of the new goal; the player walks off-screen into an unfollowed void.
**Why:** only level-01 derives its right edge from geometry.
**How to avoid:** every rebuilt level-02..08 descriptor sets `bounds.right` to the new extent (goal.x + GOAL_SIZE + buffer, matching the shipped 64–80px buffer convention). For climbs, also set `bounds.top` (e.g. −360). Verify in-engine (`browser-boot` reaches goal.x).
**Warning signs:** `browser-boot.mjs` far-end drive "stalled at x:…, never reached goal.x" (game.js check at reachedX vs goal.x > 32).

### Pitfall 3: Key placed where the driven player never touches it
**What goes wrong:** a ground/route-level key trigger inside a jump-arc landing zone is sailed clean over; static model says "reachable," real drive never collects it (34.5 Rule-1, x:760).
**Why:** the static reachability model is arc/landing-blind by design.
**How to avoid:** place the key in a WALKED stretch of the high route; confirm via `browser-boot`/audit that a driven player actually collects it.
**Warning signs:** the two-path audit's Path A shows `keyGone === false` after driving to the key.

### Pitfall 4: smoke-progress golden fixtures go stale
**What goes wrong:** `smoke-progress.mjs` deep-equals `getLevel("level-0N").geometry` against inline `expectedGeometry` fixtures. `[VERIFIED: grep, live tree]` golden fixtures exist for **levels 01, 02, 03, and 04** (assertions at lines ~398, ~495, ~608, ~748); **levels 05–08 have NO golden fixture.** Rebuilding levels 01–04 without re-baselining their fixtures HARD-FAILs the smoke; rebuilding 05–08 does not (no fixture to break).
**Why:** the fixture is a byte-for-byte regression snapshot; 34.5 hit this.
**How to avoid:** re-baseline the golden fixture for EACH of levels 01–04 in the same change that rebuilds them. Levels 05–08 need no fixture update (optionally add fixtures for them if the planner wants the same regression coverage).
**Warning signs:** `smoke-progress` "geometry must deep-equal / must match the authored descriptor" assertion fails.

### Pitfall 5: XP path divergence between math-clear and key-clear
**What goes wrong:** duplicating the clear body means one path awards XP or saves differently, or `markCleared` fires on only one path (breaking unlock derivation).
**Why:** two copies drift.
**How to avoid:** extract one `clearLevel()`; both paths call it; `markCleared` + `writeSave` in the shared function.
**Warning signs:** clearing an even level via key doesn't unlock the next level, or awards no XP.
