  <name>Task 1: ADHD-safe sound mix sign-off</name>
  <files></files>
  <action>
    Serve the game over HTTP and manually listen through the full audio system with sound on. This is a verification-only checkpoint; write no code. Follow the &lt;how-to-verify&gt; steps precisely and record the reviewer's exact response in this plan's SUMMARY.md. If a specific issue is named (e.g. the wrong-answer tone still reads as harsh, or the music is too loud relative to SFX), route the fix back to the relevant earlier plan (27-01 for a re-sourced asset, 27-02 for a CONFIG.AUDIO volume retune) and re-verify before closing.
  </action>
  <what-built>
The full v5.0 audio system: 7 distinct SFX (jump, land, correct, soft-neutral wrong, door/gate unlock, level-clear, pickup) wired at their shared mechanic seams, one calm ambient music loop that starts on the title screen's first press and never before, an M-key mute toggle with a persistent on-screen icon that works on every screen and survives a page reload, and an idempotent music manager proven (by Plan 27-06's automated browser-boot.mjs run) to never stack more than one audio element across scene transitions.
  </what-built>
  <how-to-verify>
Serve the game (cd src && python3 -m http.server 8000) and open it in a real browser with sound on — ideally headphones or real speakers, not a muted/laptop-speaker check.

1. Open a fresh/incognito window at the served URL. Confirm SILENCE before pressing anything — no music, no sound, on the title screen's initial paint.
2. Press Space/Enter/click to start. Confirm the ambient music begins immediately and reads as calm, not tense or intense.
3. Let the music loop through at least 2 full cycles (listen for a click, gap, or jarring seam at the loop point — a small imperfection is acceptable, a jarring cut is not).
4. Enter a level. Jump a few times — confirm a soft jump blip, and (if present) a soft land thud on touchdown; confirm neither sound startles or feels sharp/loud relative to the music.
5. Reach a math challenge (door, checkpoint gate, or the collect-the-answer zone). Answer WRONG on purpose at least twice — confirm the wrong-answer tone reads as calm/neutral/informational, absolutely NOT a buzzer, alarm, or startle stinger. This is the single hardest requirement in the phase — reject the mix if this fails, even if everything else sounds good.
6. Answer correctly — confirm a distinct, bright-but-soft chime, audibly different from the wrong tone.
7. Walk through a door or checkpoint gate after clearing it — confirm a distinct "unlock" cue, separate from the correct-chime.
8. Clear the level (reach the goal + answer the math gate) — confirm a calm, non-fanfare "LEVEL CLEAR" cue, distinct from both correct and door.
9. If the level has a collect-the-answer zone, touch the correct pickup — confirm a light pluck/pop, distinct from the correct chime.
10. Throughout steps 4-9, confirm the ambient music stays clearly QUIETER than every SFX in the mix — it should feel like a background bed, not compete with the sound effects.
11. Press M at any point — confirm everything (music AND SFX) goes silent immediately; press M again — confirm everything returns. Reload the page after muting — confirm the game reopens still muted (the setting persisted) and the XP/level HUD is unaffected (progress was not reset).
12. Die/respawn a few times and return to level-select via Escape, then re-enter a level — confirm the music never stacks, doubles, or phases (this is Plan 27-06's automated claim; this step is a human ear cross-check of it).
  </how-to-verify>

A vague "sounds fine" is not sufficient to close AUD-04 — name what you checked. This checkpoint does NOT auto-approve under auto_advance; a real response is required before this plan (and the phase) can close.
  </resume-signal>
