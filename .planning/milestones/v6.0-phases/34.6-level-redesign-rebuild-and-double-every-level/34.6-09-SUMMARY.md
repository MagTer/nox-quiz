# Phase 34.6 Plan 09: Rebuild level-08 (castle finale, throne-keep switchback, math-skip key) Summary

**Rebuilt `src/levels/level-08.js` from scratch as "The Throne Keep" — the game's TALLEST + most intense level and the CLIMAX of the 02→04→06→08 even-ladder (goal.x 3460 → 7620, ~2.2x). A varied castle gauntlet (portcullis door + a mandatory BARBICAN outwork climb+DESCENT over a 640px moat + a BROKEN-DRAWBRIDGE chasm crossing + 5 spikes + a castle wraith) culminates in a colossal 740px SWITCHBACK spire — two up-LEFT reversals + a diamond fork — crowned by a broad wide-right throne balcony where the GOAL sits, with the math-skip KEY on the 814px apex one tier ABOVE the throne (geometry.keys, NO locks). Mechanically distinct from level-07's monotonic staircase, and proven both ways in the real engine.**

## Deviations from Plan

**Deviation 1 [Rule 1 - Bug]: the first cut's summit goal folded OVER the switchback and never fired the end gate.** As first authored, the throne balcony SUM was an up-LEFT reversal (x:6980..7400) with the goal at x:7220 — directly ABOVE several lower keep tiers (K4/K5/K7/K9 all span that x-column). `validate-levels` AND `browser-boot` both PASSED, but `audit-endgate-key` FAILED on BOTH paths: the driver reached the goal's x on a LOWER tier and reported "arrived" (reachedX≈7211) without ever physically colliding with the goal, so Path B's end math gate never opened (challengeOpened=false) and Path A never reached the key (keyCollected=false). Fixed inline by ending the keep with an up-RIGHT hop to a wide-right throne balcony (SUM x:7360..7760, the RIGHTMOST geometry) so the goal's x-column (7620) exists ONLY at the summit — a driven player must stand on the throne to touch it (level-07's wide-right-summit S6 technique). Moved the key apex KA (7200..7440), the key (x:7280), the SUM/KA coins, checkpoints, `bounds.right` (7480→7800), and goal.x (7220→7620) to match. Re-ran: audit-endgate-key PASS both ways, coins 34/34, validate 0 HARD-FAIL. Found during Task 1; folded into commit `16b097b`. **Lesson (new pattern): a summit goal above a folding switchback is a real defect class that only the challengeOpened assertion catches — static-green + browser-boot both lie here (LEVEL-DESIGN §9.4).** The two up-LEFT main-line reversals (K6, K8) + the diamond fork + the key spur keep the switchback identity while the single up-RIGHT summit hop forces the throne.

The plan's pre-checkpoint geometry hints were explicitly SUPERSEDED by docs/LEVEL-DESIGN.md §8.5 (per the objective), so the level was authored directly to §8.5's raised bar rather than the plan's older hint geometry — this is the plan's own stated intent, not a deviation.
